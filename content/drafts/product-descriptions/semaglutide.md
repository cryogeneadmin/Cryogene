Semaglutide is a synthetic glucagon-like peptide-1 (GLP-1) receptor agonist. It is a 31-amino-acid analogue of human GLP-1(7-37) with amino acid substitutions at positions 8 and 34 and a C-18 fatty diacid chain attached at position 26 via a linker. CAS number: 910463-68-2. Molecular formula: C₁₈₇H₂₉₁N₄₅O₅₉. Molecular weight: 4113.58 g/mol.

## Research context

[DRAFT — REVIEW AND ADAPT] Semaglutide has been investigated in preclinical and clinical research for its interactions with the GLP-1 receptor. Research models have examined its effects on glucose-dependent insulin secretion, glucagon suppression, gastric emptying, and satiety signalling pathways. It appears extensively in peer-reviewed literature on metabolic regulation and GLP-1 receptor pharmacology.
